^o^ Creativity Is Much More Than 10,000 Hours of Deliberate Practice

blogs.scientificamerican - HN Discussion - Archive.is

A look into creativity as a skill and a comparison to tradition skills developed mostly through practice. Refers to the book Peak: Secrets from the New Science of Expertise by Anders Ericsson.

 

♥◡♥ Resizable Pixel Art by Marcus Blättermann

essenmitsosse.de/pixel

It's "resolution independent" pixel art! A cool experiment and a challenge to web developers to explore how responsive design can work.

 

^o^ The Journal of Design and Science

jods.mitpress.mit.edu

From the MIT Media Lab, this journal aims to merge writing and research between science and design. It's interdisciplinary in all the right ways and could be an important resource for spotting trends and industry direction as they first sprout up in a bunch of egghead imaginations.

 

≧◡≦ The Now Habit by Neil A. Fiore

GoodReads.com

I've read... no... actually... I've started to read every book to help with procrastination and time management. From Getting Things Done to Do it (Tomorrow). This is the book that clicked with me. I helps you understand the psychological benefits you get from procrastination and that understanding, of what is basically an exercise in delayed gratification like the marshmallow experiment, makes it easier to tackle.

 

♥◡♥ The 1968 Mexico Olympics

Gallery - Archive.is

One of my favorite graphic design projects, absolutely beautiful and so fully integrated with it's environment. A combo of Mexican folk art and Op-Art — it's unrivaled by any other Olympic campaign IMHO.

 

≧◡≦ UX Crash Course by Joel Marsh (2014)

Website - Archive

Joel's introduction serves a great entry to UX design. Divided up into daily lessons, it's hard to convince yourself not to check in for a month.

 

^o^ The Century of the Self

Youtube (4 hours in 3 parts)

Adam Curtis explains a wide range of topics related to the origins of modern advertising and public relations. Not graphic design focused but a fascinating introduction to the overlap between many fields including Freudian psychology, demographics and government.

 

^o^ Propaganda by Edward Bernays (1928)

Book as html - Archive.org

“Bernays’ honest and practical manual provides much insight into some of the most powerful and influential institutions of contemporary industrial state capitalist democracies... His major coup, the one that really propelled him into fame in the late 1920s, was getting women to smoke.” — Noam Chomsky

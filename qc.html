My first spark of inspiration for the Quebec design comes from a subtle piece of local history hidden in the neighborhood ironwork. A long gone ice skate manufacturer would punch blades out of iron slabs and then provide the remaining metal to be used as a decorative detail on the area's abundance of balconies. Along with the curved staircases, these architectural elements are iconic in the province.

 

The font was taken from a plaque at a historic site in Paris. It's engraved style was a useful reference for creating dimension and it's decorative French lettering would represent the prestigious status of Quebec City as the province's Capitale-Nationale.

 

The final product would merge the two and reflect the unique French Canadian working class culture that exists beyond the tourist attractions, one which stands somewhat in contrast to the city center's Old European flare.
